What is a resin printer?

Stereolithography/digital light processing (SLA/DLP) printers (also known as resin printers) are 3D printers that print using light to cure liquid resin into layers. The cheaper versions use an LCD to shine a UV image into the resin, which cures it into a solid layer. The print bed then rises slightly to let the next layer cure.

What are the pros of resin printers?

Resin allows you to print a model with fine details, far more than a standard FDM printer can produce, and the result tends to need far less post-processing work to give you a finished product.

What are the cons of resin printers?

The downside is that they tend to have smaller build plates than FDM printers, so you can't make large pieces on them. Resin also tends to be a little slower than filament as it needs to cure. You will also have to wash and clean the model afterward to get the full effect.

What items do you need in addition to the resin printer?

Wash and Cure Station, Resin, Resin Wash, Gloves, Container | Mesh Filter | Funnel, Air Filter | Mask, Mat, Long Tweezers, Super Glue, and a Magnetic attachment on build plate (optional)

What is a filament printer

Fused deposition modeling (FDM) (aka filament printer) is the style of printing most people recognize. A string of plastic called a filament is melted through a hot end and deposited on the build plate, and the next layer is then placed on top of the previous layer.

What are the pros of a filament printer?

FDM printing is used far and wide in modeling and producing larger parts like cosplay armor and helmets. The nature of the printing makes it very easy to set up and very easy to learn. Most maker spaces around the country use FDM printers as they are cheap and reliable, with plenty of options for 3D printing filament on a budget.

What are the cons of a filament printer?

When you want to make smaller models in finer detail, FDM printing can run aground. The layers will be visible as the filament prints only so finely.
